Removal of the Jellyfish Fund


Preface

As a consequence of overfishing, Jellyfish numbers have been growing at an alarming rate. Jellyfish barely contribute to the environment; they are draining the ocean of nutrients and so they are contributing damaging our climate.

Hurdles we have to jump over.

1. Closing down the unethical fisheries.

Overfishing is the main thing that we aim to stop. Our mission is to eliminate most Jellyfish in parts of the ocean because of how much of a parasite it is to the waters. Fisheries overfish, Jellyfish numbers skyrocket and then the oceans lack nutrients which is bad for the climate. Shutting down unehtical fisheries is the hardest and most likely impossible challenge.

2. Getting permits to overfish Jellyfish.

At first, this seems inane. Stopping fisheries from overfishing and then proposing that we, too overfish? However, it would actually benefit the ocean a great amount. Since Jellyfish aren't particularly helpful for the environment, it would be better if they weren't there at all, as they take up nutrients that are vital for a proper ecosystem.

This bastard.

$12 bounty for each jellyfish brought in. I hate jellyfish. Jellyfish killed my father, they're killing our planet. Because these are desperate trying times we need to take action! So $3 to whoever brings a jellyfish in, because when we face adversity, we give $1 to whoever helps.
